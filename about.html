I build and utilize tools for improving secure communication for communities that are affected by conflict. Currently, I work mainly building tools that allow users to take advantage of mesh networking technologies and the oppourtunities that pseudonimous, locality based connectivity can bring. (I build user-facing mesh networking tools)

You can find me at a bar by my giggling at publications that “solve” The Byzantine Generals problem with ever increasing CPU power, or hastely scrawling down the few solutions that might run on embedded devices. On those quieter evenings I am trying to translate dispute system design into digital spaces, or plotting a Rube Goldberg of a craft project.

I am also better at handstands than you are.
